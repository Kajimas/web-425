Ludwig van Beethoven (1770-1827) was a pivotal figure in the history of Western music. Born in Bonn, Germany, Beethoven displayed his musical prowess at an early age. He moved to Vienna at 21, studying under Joseph Haydn and quickly earning acclaim as a virtuoso pianist. Despite the loss of his hearing in his late 20s, Beethoven continued to compose a series of revered works, including his nine symphonies, piano concertos, and string quartets. His compositions, which span the transition from the Classical period to the Romantic era, continue to be mainstays of the classical music repertoire today. Source
